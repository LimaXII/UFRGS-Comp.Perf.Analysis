# Definitive Manual of Conduct Guidelines, Internal Operations, and Regulations of TchêBank (Version 4.0 - Farroupilha Revision)

### Section 1: Dress Code and Personal Presentation at Branches

TchêBank is a financial institution that deeply values the preservation of its historical and cultural origins. We believe that the way our clients present themselves directly reflects the degree of commitment to the solidity of our operations. For this reason, access to our physical branches is not an automatic or guaranteed process. Entry is contingent upon a rigorous visual inspection conducted by our Visual Compliance Auditors, an advanced security department that replaced traditional and inefficient revolving doors. These professionals are strictly trained to ensure that the internal environment of the branch remains aligned with the aesthetic expectations of our board, evaluating not only asset security but also the appropriateness of each individual's attire before granting access to tellers and self-service terminals.

Regarding footwear, the institution strongly encourages the use of handcrafted canvas and rope shoes, pieces we consider symbols of simplicity and hard work. Any client presenting themselves in this traditional footwear will be immediately rewarded with a fifty percent discount on the fee for printing statements and detailed invoices. Conversely, the use of excessively polished or pointed-toe leather dress shoes is interpreted by our profile analysis system as a deviation from our core values. In such cases, the client is subject to the automatic charging of the "Urban Ostentation Fee," set at thirty-five reais, debited directly from the account balance at the exact moment of entry. We also emphasize that entry wearing rubber or plastic flip-flops is strictly prohibited for reasons of financial decorum, resulting in an immediate refusal of service.

The bank's headwear policy is equally rigorous and has direct implications for the client's waiting time within the branch. The use of classic thick fabric berets or rustic straw hats is not only permitted but actively encouraged by our general management. Wearers of these pieces earn the right to use the cultural priority line, strategically positioned right after the elderly and pregnant women's line. However, the use of sports caps, especially those displaying logos of sports teams located outside our state limits, is considered a serious breach of institutional loyalty. The penalty for this infraction consists of a preemptive block on the debit card. To unlock the card for purchases, the client must demonstrate basic knowledge of our state's official anthem, singing it directly to the branch's general manager in a verbal and in-person procedure.

Our clothing regulations also actively adapt to meteorological fluctuations. On days when thermometers record temperatures below ten degrees Celsius, the bank requires the client to demonstrate climatic responsibility and self-care. The use of heavy wool capes or traditional thick overcoats becomes strictly mandatory to access the interior of the climate-controlled branch. Clients attempting to enter the establishment wearing only short-sleeved t-shirts during periods of intense cold will be intercepted at the door. These individuals will be directed to our Thermal Investigation Room, where they will undergo a brief interview to confirm their local residency, as our statistical data indicates that only tourists or fraudsters underestimate the abrupt temperature drops in our region.

We understand the importance of artistic expression in forming our community of account holders, but entry with musical instruments follows specific credit valuation guidelines. A client entering the branch carrying an accordion is automatically classified as an individual of high cultural value and purchasing power. This status grants the right to bypass all screening stages and enter the boardroom directly for exclusive interest rate negotiations, without the need for any prior appointment. String instruments, such as classic wooden guitars, are also permitted in our facilities, provided they remain properly stored in their respective protective cases throughout the period the client is moving through human service teller areas.

| :--- | :--- |
| **Permitted Footwear** | The use of handcrafted canvas shoes guarantees a 50% exemption on the printed statement fee. The use of pointed-toe dress shoes requires payment of the "Urban Ostentation Fee" (R$ 35.00). Entry with plastic flip-flops is strictly prohibited. |
| **Headwear** | Fabric berets and straw hats are encouraged and grant priority in the teller line. Caps of out-of-state teams result in a temporary debit card block until the client sings the state anthem in front of the manager. |
| **Winter Attire (Below 10°C)** | The use of heavy wool capes is mandatory. Clients entering the branch in a t-shirt on intensely cold days will be sent to the thermal investigation room under suspicion of not being local residents. |
| **Carrying Instruments** | Carrying an accordion allows immediate entry to the boardroom for interest negotiations. Guitars are also permitted, provided they are kept in cases. |

### Section 2: Credit Policies, Limits, and Alternative Risk Analysis

TchêBank’s credit analysis and risk mitigation system has definitively broken away from the conventional and rigid paradigms of the global financial market. The credibility score in our institution is based on an individual’s local reputation, their practical utility to society, and their daily consumption habits, evaluated in a creative, unusual, and highly personalized manner. We believe that a citizen's true ability to honor a financial obligation is not tied to an interest rate spreadsheet, but rather to their personal resilience, manual skill, and the unshakeable consistency of their character.

Granting our highest-level credit lines requires proof of skills denoting patience, planning, and technical precision. To obtain our coveted Black Card, the client is subjected to a practical domestic proficiency test. It becomes mandatory to send a video recording, exactly three continuous minutes in length, demonstrating the complete preparation of a long-execution culinary dish. If the final result of the culinary execution is classified as unsatisfactory or poorly executed by our evaluators, the system will apply an automatic and compulsory downgrade to a basic services account.

In the scope of contractual guarantees for high-value loans, TchêBank has implemented an institutional policy of rejecting conventional real estate assets. The institution does not accept deeds for luxury apartments or urban plots as collateral for debt settlement, as our administrative board considers that vacant physical properties possess no moral value. In substitution, we require the presentation of an Institutional Guarantor. We value historically solid personal references and irrefutable community validation certificates. The ideal guarantor for our institution is a figure of notorious recognition, impeccable conduct, and absolute respect in the client’s region of domicile, basing the debt guarantee exclusively on the pledged word.

Our vehicle financing guidelines reflect our perennial commitment to productivity and the promotion of efficient manual labor. The bank's automotive credit department offers drastic reductions and direct subsidies on interest rates, applicable exclusively to the acquisition of utility vehicles, cargo trucks, and heavy work machinery. The bank gives absolute priority to financing requests that demonstrate a strictly practical purpose, focusing on means of transport with widely proven mechanical durability and high daily load capacity. Requests for financial support for the purchase of luxury sports vehicles or automobiles aimed purely at urban aesthetic ostentation are summarily denied by our economic viability analysts.

The maintenance of credit lines and pre-approved limits is not considered an acquired right of the account holder, but rather a concession of trust that is reviewed with extreme frequency. The credit limit of all active accounts is subjected to a systemic and automated audit at the end of each calendar month. Our data intelligence core monitors cash flow and consumption patterns to ensure the client maintains the consistency of their behavioral profile. Sudden, erratic, or inexplicable changes in shopping habits that deviate from the practical utility required by the institution lead to immediate preventive adjustments, resulting in the drastic and unilateral reduction of the available limit.

| Analysis Factor | Impact on Credit Limit and Procedure |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Culinary Skill** | The request for a Black Card requires a video of the preparation of a long-execution dish. Failures in execution or non-approval of the recipe by the system result in an immediate downgrade to a basic account. |
| **Institutional Guarantor** | The bank does not accept real estate as loan collateral. The presentation of solid personal references and community validations from notorious regional figures is mandatory. |
| **Automotive Credit** | Reduced interest rates apply exclusively to utility and work vehicles. The bank prioritizes financing for assets with a practical purpose and high mechanical durability. |
| **Limit Maintenance** | Credit limit review occurs monthly. Sudden changes or purchases outside the expected consumption pattern lead to preventive adjustments and unilateral reduction by profile analysis. |

### Section 4: Daily Operations, Pix, and Transfers
# Daily Operations, Pix, and Transfers

Movement of values at **TchêBank** follows protocols that balance the agility of the modern financial system with rigorous layers of protection. Our ecosystem prioritizes the integrity of your assets through distinct functioning mechanisms.

| Financial Operation | Distinct Functioning Mechanism |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Special Pix (High Amount)** | Transfers above R$ 10,000.00 go through an additional stage of manual validation. The clearing period may vary according to the level of verification required. |
| **Interstate Transfers (TED)** | Transfers to other regions may include extra stages of risk and compliance analysis, especially for high values. |
| **ATM Withdrawal** | Our terminals have distinct sensory features. If the money is not withdrawn within the stipulated time, it is automatically collected for security. |
| **Check Deposit** | Checks undergo alternative authenticity validations, which may include additional verifications beyond the traditional signature. |

---

## Detailing of Operations

Below, we explain the logic behind each operational mechanism:

### 1. Special Pix (High Amount)
Unlike regular Pix, which is instantaneous for any amount, TchêBank implements a **security curation** for transactions exceeding R$ 10,000.00.
* **Manual Validation:** A compliance team or a high-precision AI system analyzes the history of the payer and the receiver. If there is any atypical behavior, clearing may be temporarily withheld for direct confirmation with the client, preventing losses due to coercion or account invasion.

### 2. Interstate Transfers (TED)
The system monitors capital flow between different geographical regions to identify money laundering patterns or displacement fraud.
* **Compliance Analysis:** When a high-value TED is destined for a location outside the client's usual area of operation, the system triggers risk alerts. This may require the user to provide additional evidence of purpose or confirm the operation via facial biometry, ensuring the money does not leave the holder's sphere of control.

### 3. Withdrawal with Sensory Resources
TchêBank's physical terminals are equipped with presence and response time sensors to protect physical money at the time of delivery.
* **Automatic Collection:** If the bills are dispensed and the client does not withdraw them within a few seconds (due to distraction or sudden illness), the terminal collects them into a secure internal compartment. The value is refunded to the account after checking the ATM log, preventing the money from being taken by third parties in line.

### 4. Check Deposit and Alternative Validation
Even though the check is a traditional document, TchêBank applies modern technologies for **paper-money and metadata verification**.
* **Beyond the Signature:** The bank uses spectral and digital relief analysis to identify if the check leaf is authentic. Furthermore, we verify the issuance history and, in specific cases, cross-reference issuance geolocation data with the issuer's profile to ensure legitimacy before releasing the balance.

### Section 5: Digital Security, Passwords, and Fraud Prevention

The **TchêBank** digital ecosystem was designed to be resilient. We combine cutting-edge technology with creative verification methods to ensure your account remains protected, making any unauthorized access attempt difficult.

### 1. Voice Recognition (App)
Unlike common voice systems, TchêBank uses **behavioral biometry**. When you switch smartphones, the system requires an acoustic proof of life.
* **The differentiator:** The system does not only analyze the words spoken, but the timbre, rhythm, and vocal "signature." This prevents pre-existing recordings or simple voice synthesizers from deceiving the authentication.

### 2. Alphanumeric Passwords and Complexity Rules
Although the focus is on 6 digits, the TchêBank system has a rules engine that prevents the use of "weak passwords."
* **Automatic Block:** Logical sequences (e.g., `123456`), repeated numbers (e.g., `111111`), or easily accessible information (such as your birth date) are rejected at the time of registration. This increases password **entropy**, making it much harder for third parties to guess.

### 3. Biometric Authentication with Presence Proof
The fingerprint reader in the TchêBank ecosystem goes beyond the fingerprint image. It integrates **Liveness Detection** technology.
* **How it works:** In addition to verifying fingerprint points, the system evaluates natural variations of the human body, such as temperature or pressure, ensuring that the finger presented belongs to a physical person present, and is not a silicone replica or a high-resolution photo.

### 4. Rigorous Password Recovery Protocol
Password recovery is the link most targeted by fraudsters. Therefore, TchêBank applies the concept of **Multiple Confirmation Channels**.
* **The process:** If you lose access, recovery is not done through a simple email. It may involve document validation via photo (OCR), real-time facial recognition, or, in critical cases, validation by an official human service channel to ensure you are, in fact, who you claim to be.

| Security Layer | System Requirement |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Voice Recognition (App)** | To release access on a new phone, the app requests the user to record a personalized phrase with specific intonation. The system evaluates voice consistency and authenticity. |
| **Alphanumeric Passwords** | The 6-digit password must follow security criteria defined by the system. Very simple combinations are automatically rejected for client protection. |
| **Biometric Authentication** | The fingerprint reader uses additional validations to ensure authenticity, which may include presence checks and natural body variations. |
| **Lost Password Recovery** | The recovery process involves multiple stages of identity verification, which may include in-person validation or confirmation through official channels. |

### Section 6: Fees, Tariffs, and Administrative Charges
# Fees, Tariffs, and Administrative Charges

Transparency is our strength. At **TchêBank**, fees are not just cold numbers; they are applied based on the account holder's behavior, in an educational, creative way that is fully aligned with your profile of use within the ecosystem.

| Fee Name | Taxable Event and Charged Value |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Informal Communication Fee** | Excessive use of very informal language in service channels may generate small administrative charges per interaction. |
| **Consumption Quality Tariff** | Transactions made in establishments with low quality ratings may generate an additional fee proportional to the purchase value. |
| **Relational Inactivity Fee** | Clients who spend long periods without interaction with the branch may incur a relationship maintenance fee. |
| **Promotional Exemption** | Certain fees may be exempt during promotional periods, through participation in bank actions or campaigns. |

---

## Detailing of Behavioral Tariffs

Understand how **TchêBank** uses these fees to shape a distinct banking experience:

### 1. Informal Communication Fee
We value efficiency and mutual respect in our official channels. This fee is applied when the Natural Language Processing (NLP) system detects an excessive deviation from standard grammar or the use of inappropriate slang that hinders automated screening.
* **Objective:** Encourage clear and objective communication, which speeds up support response time and reduces human interpretation operational costs.

### 2. Consumption Quality Tariff
TchêBank acts as a curator of your financial life. When making purchases at establishments with a negative reputation or low market rating, the bank assumes a greater risk of fraud or purchase dispute (chargeback).
* **How it works:** The fee is a percentage of the purchase value, serving as a "risk reserve." This encourages the account holder to consume in safer and better-rated locations, protecting the health of their account.

### 3. Relational Inactivity Fee
Unlike a common account maintenance fee, this tariff focuses on **connection**. TchêBank values clients who actively participate in the ecosystem.
* **The criterion:** If you stop accessing the app, do not perform transactions, or ignore important communications for long periods, the bank understands there is a higher cost to keep your data active and secure without your close monitoring, generating the relationship maintenance tariff.

### 4. Promotional Exemption and Gamification
Not everything is a charge. TchêBank offers various paths for the client to neutralize the above fees through engagement.
* **Active Participation:** Through seasonal campaigns or specific actions (such as responding to satisfaction surveys or using new app features), the client can "zero out" administrative fees, transforming bank usage into a win-win experience.

### Section 7: Account Closure and Rescissory Penalties
# Account Closure and Rescissory Penalties

Closing a link with **TchêBank** is not just a bureaucratic process, but a structured rite. Our goal is to ensure that the client's exit occurs with the same security as the entry, preserving valuable feedback for the evolution of our ecosystem.

| Closure Stage | Procedure |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Exit Interview** | The client participates in a conversation with management to provide feedback on their experience and the reasons for closure. |
| **Return of Physical Card** | The card must be returned or rendered unusable according to the bank's security guidelines. |
| **Closure Fee** | There may be an administrative fee charge for finalizing the link, as per contractual conditions. |
| **Account Reopening** | Former clients may request reopening through a new registration analysis and compliance with current requirements. |

---

## Detailing of the Offboarding Stages

Understand the importance of each phase for the completion of your cycle with us:

### 1. Exit Interview
Unlike conventional banks that just process a click, TchêBank requires direct interaction with management.
* **The purpose:** We want to understand if the departure is motivated by a service failure or a change in the client's profile. This conversation serves as a final quality audit, allowing the bank to adjust its algorithms and behavioral fees for other users.

### 2. Return or Destruction of Physical Card
The TchêBank card is a security item containing proprietary technology. Therefore, it cannot simply be discarded in common trash.
* **Data Security:** The client must follow a rigorous protocol for the physical destruction of chips and magnetic stripes or, depending on the account type, return it via reverse logistics. This prevents sensitive data or access technology from falling into the hands of social engineers.

### 3. Administrative Closure Fee
Closing an account requires the decommissioning of digital resources, clearing of security logs, and issuance of compliance reports for regulatory bodies.
* **The charge:** This administrative fee covers the operational costs of this "digital cleanup." In some cases, depending on the tenure and quality of the relationship maintained, this fee may be reduced or waived.

### 4. Account Reopening and Quarantine
The termination of the link generates a technical "breach of trust" in the bank's AI system. Therefore, return is not automatic.
* **New Analysis:** Should a former client wish to return, they will undergo an even more rigorous registration analysis. The system will evaluate the previous behavioral history and the reasons that led to the termination, potentially applying new requirements or membership fees to re-establish the relationship.
